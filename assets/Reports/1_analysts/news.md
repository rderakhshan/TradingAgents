## Executive Summary

This past week witnessed a notable pullback in SPY as a confluence of headwinds emerged: **resurgent inflation fears**, a **geopolitically charged environment** (Iran conflict, China summit disappointment), a **historic Fed leadership transition** (Warsh replacing Powell), and an **oil price surge**. SPY fell ~0.9–1.2% on multiple sessions, bond yields rose, and technical analysts are flagging elevated correction risk heading into summer. However, strong Q1 earnings growth provides a fundamental floor.

---

## 1. FED & MONETARY POLICY — Watershed Transition

**The single biggest macro event of the week** is the confirmation that **Kevin Warsh will replace Jerome Powell as Fed Chair**. This marks a profound shift in monetary policy leadership. Key details:

- The federal funds rate has held at **3.75% since December 2025** (after three 0.25% cuts in fall 2025).
- The **10-year Treasury yield sits at 4.46%** (as of May 13), up 0.16% from a month ago — a significant move that signals rising inflation expectations and/or term premium.
- Barron's graded Powell's Fed as "Good for Stocks, Bad for Affordability," acknowledging the trade-off inherent in the easy-money era.
- The transition to Warsh introduces **policy uncertainty**: while markets expect a pro-business/pro-growth stance, the timing amid an Iran conflict and inflation pickup raises questions about the pace of future cuts.

**Implication for SPY:** Rate-sensitive sectors (financials, real estate, utilities) face re-pricing risk as the market digests what a Warsh-led Fed means for the rate path.

---

## 2. INFLATION — Re-accelerating Pressures

Multiple data points confirm inflation is re-heating:

- **"Stocks Slide as Inflation Fears Lift Bond Yields"** — SPY down 0.91–1.17% on multiple sessions.
- **Shoe/footwear prices rising** across multiple articles — a consumer-staples leading indicator — driven by oil costs and the Iran conflict.
- **Oil price surge** is feeding through to input costs across the economy (gas → fashion retail → general consumer goods).
- Ben Carlson's commentary advises hedging real-world inflation via asset selection (skip luxury goods, house-hunt smart).

**Implication for SPY:** Inflation worries directly undermine the Fed's ability to cut rates, which was the primary bull-case narrative. If inflation stays sticky, the terminal rate stays higher for longer, compressing equity valuations.

---

## 3. GEOPOLITICS — Iran War & China Summit Disappointment

Two major geopolitical pressures weighed on sentiment:

- **Iran War:** Explicitly cited as "adding fuel to the fire" for rising prices. Oil markets are repricing risk, hurting equities broadly.
- **China Summit (ended ~May 13–14):** Markets had expected China to commit to purchasing more US oil (Alaskan specifically). The summit ended **"sans major commitments,"** causing pre-market slides. This dashed hopes of a US-China trade/diplomatic thaw.

**Implication for SPY:** Geopolitical risk premium is rising. Energy stocks benefit (higher oil), but the broader index suffers from input cost pressure, consumer spending drag, and uncertainty discount.

---

## 4. TECHNICAL WARNING — Overextended & Due for Mean Reversion

A critical technical call comes from **Lance Roberts of RIA Advisors** (via Thoughtful Money podcast):

> *"The S&P 500 has stretched so far above its key moving averages that a mean-reversion move could be a higher-probability scenario heading into the summer... a 10% correction this summer is not out of the question."*

With SPY near highs and the deviation from moving averages described as "massive," the risk of a summer pullback is elevated. This is a **cautionary signal** for near-term positioning.

---

## 5. INTERNATIONAL ROTATION — Capital Fleeing US Markets?

A notable trend: **International developed markets are crushing the S&P 500** over the past year. The Vanguard FTSE Developed Markets ETF (VEA) returned **34%** versus the S&P 500's lagging performance. This suggests a sustained rotation out of US mega-cap dominance toward undervalued international equities.

**Implication for SPY:** If this rotation continues, SPY could face persistent outflows, capping upside even if fundamentals are sound.

---

- **Strong Q1 earnings growth** across Tech, Energy, and Retail sectors.
- **Nvidia and Walmart earnings loom** — these are two of the most consequential reports for SPY. Nvidia represents AI/tech heavyweight leadership; Walmart represents consumer health. Both will set the tone for the next leg.
- SPY itself yields under 2%, pushing income investors toward alternatives.
