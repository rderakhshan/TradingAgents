## SPY Sentiment Report (2026-05-10 to 2026-05-17)

### 1. Overall Sentiment Direction: **Bearish** (moderate confidence)

All three data sources converge on a bearish tone. News headlines emphasize inflation jitters, rising bond yields, and the risk of a summer correction. Retail sentiment on StockTwits leans slightly bearish/anxious, with heavy geopolitical chatter. Reddit engagement confirms the bearish skew through losses, put plays, and pullback bets. The only bullish voices are isolated (e.g., a few lucky call trades) and lack institutional backing. Confidence is moderate due to the relatively small sample sizes (30 StockTwits messages, ~13 Reddit posts) and the dominance of macro/political noise rather than earnings fundamentals.

### 3. Divergences, Alignments, and Key Narratives

- **Cross-source alignment**: All three sources agree on bearish sentiment. News provides the macro rationale (inflation → yields up → stocks down), StockTwits adds geopolitical fear (Iran/China), and Reddit confirms real trader pain (losses, put buying).
- **Divergence**: A slight retail/WSB bullish streak exists (the Powell farewell gain, some OTM call wins), but it is dwarfed by the bearish engagement and lacks institutional backing.
- **Dominant narrative**: Rising yields + inflation + geopolitical uncertainty = elevated risk of a pullback. The Chinese summit ended without major trade commitments, and the prospect of U.S.-Iran conflict adds an overnight tail risk. Many traders are positioning defensively or taking profits.
